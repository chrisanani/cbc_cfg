Counseling Skills

  1. Ability to be a clear and effective communicator.

  2. Ability to ascertain early enough the real need of a potential counselee.

  3. Ability to win trust by counselees.

  4. Ability to understand and appreciate the feelings or mentality of the young. Remember your past and appreciate their situation.

  5. Humility to admit that you could be limited in some way without creating hopelessness.

  6. Develop the ability to create rapport [establish some level of friendship / relationship].

  7. Ability to pay attention.

  8. Ability to listen and understand.

  9. Ability to observe and interpret [posture, facial expression, voice, any other body language].

  10. Ability to discern.

  11. Ability to question appropriately [know when, how, where and why to ask what]. Avoid close-ended questions that require yes or no answers because you will not get enough information.

  12. Ability to summarize. You need it to let counselee know you have understood him/her.

  13. Ability to paraphrase what you hear as way of ensuring good understanding.

  14. Ability to confront [without offending] counselee with hard facts and truth, John 3:3.

  15. Ability to probe without offending.

  16. Ability to say no to unacceptable request – E.g following a married man/woman.

  17. Ability to warn [without threatening / frightening [1 Thess. 5: 14; 2 Thess. 3: 11 – 13].

  18. Ability to be an emotional support for them.

  19. Ability to play these ROLES:
    R = relax the client by using facial expressions that show interest.
    O = open up the client using warm and caring tone of voice so he/she can talk.
    L = lean towards the client and not away from him/her.
    E = establish and maintain eye contact.
    S = smile much.
