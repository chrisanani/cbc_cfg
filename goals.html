Goals of counseling

    These include but not limited to the following:

  1. Helping counselee to know Christ more in his/her situation [good or bad one].

  2. Helping him make rational decisions.

  3. Making counselee grounded and matured in Christ [i.e. independent, Eph. 4:13]

  4. Making the counselee a future counselor, 2 Tim 2:2; 2 Cor. 1:4.

  5. Changing the counseleeâ€™s behavior, attitudes or values positively. Misconceptions corrected.

  6. Preventing more serious problems from developing.

  7. Teaching spiritual and social skills or disciplines.

  8. Encouraging expression of emotions [in a positive way].

  9. Creating an environment of support in times of need.

  10. Helping counselee to identify, discover and exploit their personal potentials.

  11. personal responsibility in him/her.
