Follow–up of Counselees

What is Follow – up?

Follow–up is a process of giving continued attention to new converts or counselees until they are established in Christ Jesus and the Church by finding out, their gifts, developing them and deploying them. Follow–up is not only meant for new converts but also for Christians who visit the Church for the first time.

Follow-Up in Four Areas

Our Follows are in four areas. They are mostly the counselees we receive.

  1. accept Jesus Christ as their Lord and Personal Saviour.
  2. want to join the Church
  3. come to rededicate their lives to Christ.
  4. come for prayer.

All these four groups need to be followed – up. The question is, are they all going to be followed – up in the same way? The answer is simply a big NO. Their needs are different. This writeup is to help to bring to the fore these differences and to devise effective ways by which effective follow – up can be made.
The follow – ups by the counsellors are by two methods namely individual follow–up and mass follow–up.

Individual Follow – up
The counsellor will within 48hrs make the first contact with the counselee either by phone or by personal visit to the home/Workplace of the counselee.

Mass Follow – up

The ministry organizes mass follow – up to enable personal visits to be made to counselees who weren’t visited in a quarter. The members are put into groups and sent to particular areas where these counselees live.
In all these phone calls or visits, the counsellor will say that “the senior pastor” has sent him/her to the counselee.

What is the importance of Follow – up?

Through follow-up, counselees are encouraged to get closer to God and their self-worth increases. Follow–ups are channels where an individual would be church members receive love, care, attention, nurturing, encouragement, and finally make their decision to stay in the Church or not; (1 Thessalonians 2:7-14).
The mindset of the counsellor in Follow-up is to mature them in Christ.
28 So we tell others about Christ, warning everyone and teaching everyone with all the wisdom God has given us. We want to present them to God, perfect in their relationship to Christ. 29 That's why I work and struggle so hard, depending on Christ's mighty power that works within me. Col 1:28-29.
One other reason why Follow -up is important, is that as newborn babies or visitors who come to the Church, they may not be familiar with and also not have friends in the Church so through Follow – up, they can strike a friendship with the counsellor.
Through prayer and teaching we are to protect them from that evil one, Satan the devil, from his deception and discouragement; (1 Samuel 17:34-36; Jeremiah 23:4).

What qualities do the counsellor need to do effective Follow -up? - John 10:1-16.

  • Must earn the role as a Counsellor vrs 1 - 2

  • Ability to instruct and lead the sheep/Counselee

  • Availability

  • Ability to provide food for the counselee/sheep

  • Ability to bring lost sheep back to the fold


What does Follow – up involve?

Visit
In visits, we mean the counsellor makes an appointment with the counselee and honours it by going to the agreed venue of the counselee either in their homes or places of work or any convenient place of the counselee.

Phone calls
As the name suggests, the counsellor will make a personal call to the counselee within 48hrs of counselling.

Prayer
At the time of counselling, the counsellor might observe certain things about the counselee as well as the prayer request, he/she made put all these into prayer on behalf of the counselee,

2 Thess 2:13-14 13 But we ought always to thank God for you, brothers loved by the Lord, because from the beginning God chose you to be saved through the sanctifying work of the Spirit and belief in the truth. 14 He called you to this through our gospel, that you might share in the glory of our Lord Jesus Christ.

  • When you call a counselee, you will say the senior Pastor has asked me to call you and to find out how you are doing.

  • When you visit, the senior pastor has sent you to personally visit.

  • Decline food, drink etc politely except for water. Better still you hold your bottled water to the house of your counselee.


Messages to carry to your counselee

What message can you carry to your counselee? those who

1. accept Jesus Christ as their Lord and Personal Saviour,
  • Assure the counselee of salvation. Romans 10: 9 – 10
  • Encourage the counselee to have continuous fellowship with the other believers.
  • Encourage them to attend Church and discipleship classes and be baptized.
  • Encourage the counselee to pray always to avoid falling into temptation.
  • Encourage the counselee to have daily quiet time or walk with God. Col 2:6.
  • Introduce the counselee to the vision, mission, sheepfold, prayer meeting, Thursday
    restoration hour, and other activities of the church.
  • Ask for their concerns or prayer request.

2. want to join the Church
  • Assure the counselee of joining a Bible-believing Church where he/she will be well fed.
  • Introduce the counselee to the vision, mission, sheepfold, prayer meeting, Thursday restoration hour, and other activities of the church.
  • Encourage the counselee to attend the discipleship class to be baptized or voted into membership.
  • Ask for their concerns or prayer request.

3. come to rededicate their lives to Christ
  • Assure the counselee that God truly loves him/her citing the prodigal son as an example.
  • Warn the backslider of the dangers of backsliding. Romans 2: 22 – 24. One gives opportunities to the enemies of God to blaspheme him.
  • Ask the counselee to avoid activities that lead to backslidings but make sure there is a total turnaround.
  • Encourage the counselee to have constant fellowship with God through studying God’s word, prayer, worship and witness.
  • Encourage the counselee to have faith in God for delaying doesn’t mean God has neglected or abandoned them.

4. come for prayer
  • Assure the counselee that God hears and honours persistent prayers. Luke 18:1
  • Assure the counselee of God’s constant love and protection. (Romans 8:35-39).
  • Introduce the counselee to prayer meetings, Thursday restoration hour, and other activities of the church.
  • Ask about the prayer request made.

Steps to effective follow-up
  • Pray for the counselee before embarking on follow–up
  • Know what you are going to say to your counselee.
  • When you get the counselee through phone calls or personal visits, after the introduction of yourself, you tell them the senior Pastor has asked you to make the call or the visit.
  • Pray for the counselee.
  • Know you interact with the counselees after asking if he/she can spare you some time to talk.
  • Find out his/her well being.
  • Discuss your message with him/her based on the decision made in Church.
  • Ask about any concerns.

Follow-Up Tips
  • Intercede for them. (1 Thessalonians 5:18).
  • Pay constant visits to him/her; be very close to them.
  • Counsel/advise them frequently.
  • Develop an atmosphere of loving concern. (Proverbs 17:17).
  • Develop your relationship with them around Christ. (1 John 1:3).
  • Be patient and persevering; (go on trying). (1 Thessalonians 2:1-7).
  • Spend quality time together with them. (Proverbs 18:24).
  • Be interested in more than just their spiritual life. Present gifts to them as occasion necessitates within your ability.
  • Remember what they tell you.
  • Keep their secrets secret.
